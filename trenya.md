# Training Plan

## Daily Warm-up (5-8 min)

Do this before each workout:

- 20 arm circles each direction
- Shoulder dislocations with a towel
- Cat-Cow x10
- Thoracic rotations x10
- Hip flexor stretch
- Deep squat hold, 30 sec
- Scapular pull-ups x10
- Band pull-aparts if you buy a resistance band

Note: resistance bands are highly recommended.

## Weekly Schedule

### Monday: Pull + Core

- Pull-ups: 4 sets
  - 8-12 reps: regular pull-ups
  - Fewer reps: assisted pull-ups or negatives
- Australian rows (low bar): 4x10-15
  - Horizontal pulling exercise done under a low bar; improves upper-back strength and posture.
- Scapular pull-ups: 3x12
  - Small pull-up motion using only the shoulder blades; teaches scapular control and helps shoulder health.
- Hanging knee raises: 4x12
- Dead hang: 3x30-60 sec
  - Passive hang from the bar to decompress the spine, improve grip, and open the shoulders.
- Side plank: 3x40 sec each side

Notes:

- Australian rows are very important for posture.
- Scapular pull-ups are excellent for rounded shoulders.
- Dead hangs are good for shoulders and spine.
- This day is intentionally grip-heavy, but it should not feel like grip survival training. If hands or forearms fail before the back, reduce hanging volume.

Easier grip option:

- Pull-ups: 4 sets
- Australian rows: 4x10-15
- Scapular pull-ups: 2x10
- Hanging knee raises: 3x10-12
- Dead hang: 2x20-40 sec
- Side plank: 3x40 sec each side

If hands still get overloaded, replace hanging knee raises with reverse crunches or plank.

### Tuesday: Push + Posture

- Push-ups: 4x12-20
  - Progression: incline -> regular -> decline -> diamond
- Pike push-ups: 4x8-12
  - Bodyweight vertical pressing pattern that targets the shoulders more than standard push-ups.
- Bench or parallel bar dips, if available: 3x10-15
- Wall slides: 3x15
  - Stand against a wall and slide the arms upward while keeping contact; helps shoulder mobility and posture.
- Prone Y-T-W raises: 3 rounds
  - Lie face down and raise the arms in Y, T, and W shapes to strengthen the upper back and rear shoulders.

Notes:

- Pike push-ups build the shoulders.
- Prone Y-T-W raises are excellent for posture.
- Stretch your chest afterward.

### Wednesday: Rest / Recovery

- Easy walk, 30-60 min
- Light mobility if stiff
- Daily posture fix routine

Note: this is a full recovery day. Keep it easy.

### Thursday: Legs + Core

- Bulgarian split squat: 4x12 each leg
  - Rear foot elevated split squat that builds quads, glutes, balance, and single-leg strength.
- Walking lunges: 3x20
- Single-leg Romanian deadlift (bodyweight): 3x12
  - Hip-hinge on one leg that trains hamstrings, glutes, and balance.
- Calf raises: 4x20
  - Simple lower-leg movement to strengthen the calves and ankle stability.
- Reverse crunch: 3x15
  - Core exercise focused on curling the pelvis upward to target the lower abs.
- Bird dogs: 3x15
  - Opposite arm-and-leg extension that trains core stability and spinal control.

Finish with 10-15 minutes of mobility work.

Note: single-leg Romanian deadlifts are excellent for balance.

### Friday: Pull + Arms

- Chin-ups: 4 sets to max reps
  - Underhand-grip pull-up variation that emphasizes the biceps and lats.
- Neutral-grip pull-ups, if the bar allows: 3x8-10
- Australian rows: 3x15
- Towel curls: 3x12
  - Curling movement using a towel around a bar; mimics a biceps curl with bodyweight resistance.
- Hanging leg raises: 3x10
  - Advanced hanging core movement that trains the abs and hip flexors more than knee raises.
- Dead hang: 3x1 min
  - Longer passive hang for grip endurance, shoulder mobility, and spinal decompression.

Towel curl setup:

- Loop a towel around a sturdy bar.
- Lean back to create resistance.
- Curl your body toward the bar.

Note: chin-ups are especially effective for biceps growth.

### Saturday: Gym (Heavy Full Body)

This is the main muscle-building day.

- Squat: 4x5-8
- Romanian deadlift: 3x8
- Overhead press: 4x6-8
  - Standing press overhead that builds shoulders, triceps, and upper-body pressing strength.
- Weighted pull-ups or lat pulldown: 4x8
- Bench press or incline dumbbell press: 4x6-8
- Decline push-ups: 3x10-15
  - Push-up variation with feet elevated to shift more load to the upper chest and shoulders.
- Pseudo-planche push-ups: 2x8
  - Lean-forward push-up variation that increases shoulder, chest, and triceps demand.
- Hollow body hold: 3x30 sec
  - Gymnastics-style core hold that builds deep abdominal tension and body control.
- Superman hold: 3x30 sec
  - Back-extension hold that strengthens the lower back, glutes, and posterior chain.
- Face pulls: 3x15
- Lateral raises: 4x15
- Biceps curls: 3x12
- Triceps pushdown: 3x12

Notes:

- Overhead press is one of the best lifts for wider shoulders.
- If you want conventional deadlifts, replace Romanian deadlifts with barbell deadlifts for 3x3-5.
- Face pulls are one of the best exercises for posture.
- Lateral raises are critical for shoulder width.
- Superman holds are great for the lower back.

### Sunday: Rest / Recovery

- 45-60 min walk
- Mobility work
- Stretch chest
- Stretch hip flexors
- Thoracic mobility
  - Focus on upper-back rotation and extension drills to counter stiffness from sitting.
- Foam rolling, if available
  - Use a foam roller on tight areas like upper back, glutes, quads, and calves to reduce stiffness.

## Daily Posture Fix Routine (10 min)

Do this every day, including rest days, especially if you sit a lot:

- Chin tucks x15
  - Gently pull the head straight back to make a "double chin" without tilting up or down; strengthens deep neck flexors and helps forward-head posture.
- Wall slides x15
- Cat-Cow x10
- Thoracic extensions x10
  - Extend the upper back over a bench, chair back, or foam roller; improves thoracic extension and reduces slouched posture.
- Scapular pull-ups x10
- Dead hang x60 sec
- Hip flexor stretch, 1 min each side
- Chest doorway stretch, 1 min

Note: consistency matters more here than intensity.

## Nutrition

Priority: fat loss while preserving or building muscle.

- Aim to lose about 0.4-0.7 kg per week.
- Eat 1.6-2.2 g of protein per kg of body weight.
- At 90 kg, a good target is about 160-190 g protein per day.
- Maintain a moderate calorie deficit of around 300-500 kcal per day.
- Eat plenty of vegetables and fruit.
- Drink 2.5-3 L of water daily.
- Sleep 7.5-9 hours if possible.

## Daily Meal Plan Example (90 kg)

This is a simple fat-loss example with high protein. Adjust portions based on your weekly weight trend.

### Breakfast

- 250 g Greek yogurt
- 60 g oats
- 1 banana
- 20 g nuts

Approx: 35 g protein

### Lunch

- 180-200 g chicken breast
- 150 g cooked rice
- Large salad with olive oil
- 1 fruit

Approx: 45-50 g protein

### Snack

- Protein shake
- 2 boiled eggs

Approx: 35-40 g protein

### Dinner

- 180-220 g lean beef, turkey, or fish
- Potatoes or rice
- Cooked vegetables

Approx: 45-50 g protein

### Before Bed, if needed

- 200 g cottage cheese or skyr

Approx: 20-25 g protein

Daily total: about 180-195 g protein

Notes:

- If fat loss is too slow for 2 weeks, reduce carbs or fats slightly.
- If energy is too low, add a bit more carbs around training.
- Put the largest carb meals before or after training if that helps performance.

## Best Time To Train

The best time is the time you can follow consistently.

Practical rules:

- Train 2-3 hours after a full meal when possible.
- If training in the morning, eat something light first if performance feels low.
- Late morning to early evening is usually best for strength and energy.
- Avoid very late training if it hurts sleep.

## Progression

Each week, try to improve in one of these ways:

- Add 1 rep per set.
- Add 1 extra set.
- Use a harder exercise variation.
- Reduce rest time slightly while maintaining good form.

When you can comfortably do 12-15 clean pull-ups or 20+ push-ups per set, move to harder variations or add weight with something like a backpack or dip belt.

## Useful Equipment

If you buy only two things, get these:

- Resistance bands for face pulls, rows, shoulder work, and assisted pull-ups
- Gymnastic rings for rows, dips, push-ups, curls, triceps extensions, and other movements that are hard to do with just a bar

Notes:

- Both are inexpensive and portable.
- Together they make outdoor training much more versatile.

## Expected Results

If you train consistently, eat enough protein, and keep a moderate calorie deficit:

- After 6-8 weeks: stronger core, better posture, improved pull-up numbers, and less back discomfort from sitting
- After 3-4 months: more visible shoulder and arm development, a slimmer waist, and a clearer V-taper
- After 6-12 months: losing around 8-12 kg of fat while building muscle can significantly change your physique, especially if you keep the weekly heavy gym session

## Summary

This combination of 5 training days, 2 recovery days, one focused heavy gym session each week, daily posture work, and solid nutrition is a sustainable way to improve physique, posture, and long-term back health.
